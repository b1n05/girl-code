What are Sharks?

Sharks are a type of fish characterized by their cartilaginous skeletons (made of cartilage, not bone) and streamlined bodies. They are a diverse group with over 500 known species, found in all oceans and some freshwater habitats. Sharks are known for their predatory nature and play a crucial role in maintaining healthy marine ecosystems.

Lemon Sharks

The lemon shark is a species of shark from the family Carcharhinidae, known for its yellowish skin, which inspires its common name. It is classified as a Vulnerable species by the International Union for the Conservation of Nature. Lemon sharks can grow to 3.4 metres in length.

Hammerhead Shark

The hammerhead sharks are a group of sharks that form the family Sphyrnidae, named for the unusual and distinctive form of their heads, which are flattened and laterally extended into a cephalofoil.

Whale Shark

The whale shark is a slow-moving, filter-feeding carpet shark and the largest known extant fish species. The largest confirmed individual had a length of 18.8 m. The whale shark holds many records for size in the animal kingdom, most notably being by far the most massive living non-cetacean animal.
